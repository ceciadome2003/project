# Selected Topic & Identifying Gaps

---

### 🌸 Why La Primavera by Botticelli?
Sandro Botticelli's *Primavera* (painted around 1480) is one of the most fascinating and complex iconographical puzzles of the Italian Renaissance. Deeply intertwined with the **[Neoplatonic philosophy](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neoplatonismo)** promoted by Marsilio Ficino and the Medici court, the painting is not merely a decorative spring scene. It represents a spiritual journey where Love acts as a cosmic force, driving the human soul from earthly passion up to intellectual contemplation.

The artwork features **nine specific mythological characters** arranged in an orange grove:
1. **Zephyrus**: The blueish spring wind representing primordial physical passion.
2. **Chloris**: The nymph chased by Zephyrus, who transforms through love.
3. **Flora**: The full metamorphosis of Chloris into the goddess of Spring, scattering flowers.
4. **Venus**: The central axis (*Venus Humanitas*), representing moral harmony and spiritual balance.
5. **Cupid**: Hovering above Venus, blindfolded, aiming his arrow at the Graces.
6. **The Three Graces**: Representing *Voluptas* (Pleasure), *Castitas* (Chastity), and *Pulchritudo* (Beauty) in a neoplatonic triad.
7. **Mercury**: At the far left, dispelling clouds with his caduceus, representing Reason and celestial knowledge.

### 🔍 Identifying the Knowledge Gap
While institutional catalogs are excellent at documenting the physical properties of artworks (such as dimensions, materials, or authors), they frequently suffer from **scarcity in denotative and allegorical data**.

By exploring the **ArCo Knowledge Graph**, our team discovered a significant informational void:
* The painting exists in ArCo under the resource IRI `https://w3id.org/arco/resource/HistoricOrArtisticProperty/0900158550`.
* However, the graph only describes its subject with a single, flat literal string: **"Allegoria Della Primavera"**.
* **The Gap**: There is absolutely no structured reference to the nine characters, their positions, or their profound neoplatonic allegories within the triples. 

Our project directly addresses this gap by extracting structured knowledge via LLMs and translating it into ArCo-compliant RDF triples.
