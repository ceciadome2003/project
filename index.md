# Enriching Cultural Heritage Knowledge Graphs
## A Semantic Approach to Botticelli's Primavera using SPARQL and LLMs

---

### 📝 Project Introduction
This project was developed within the framework of the **KE4H (Knowledge Engineering for the Humanities)** course at the **[University of Bologna](https://www.unibo.it/it)**. Our primary goal was to explore how the combination of advanced SPARQL queries and Large Language Models (LLMs) can be effectively used to identify and enrich an existing cultural heritage knowledge graph with new, meaningful, and deeply contextual content.

We chose to focus on **[Sandro Botticelli's masterpiece, "La Primavera"](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primavera_(Botticelli))**, a monumental Renaissance painting located in the Uffizi Gallery. Despite its immense historical, philosophical, and artistic value, its detailed iconographical representation in structured knowledge graphs remains limited. This made it a perfect candidate for semantic enrichment through AI-powered language models.

As our base knowledge graph, we selected **[ArCo](http://wit.istc.cnr.it/arco/)**, the official ontology network and RDF dataset of the Italian Ministry of Culture, which provides an excellent SPARQL endpoint for cultural heritage exploration.
